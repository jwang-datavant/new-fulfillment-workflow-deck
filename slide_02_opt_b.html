Slide 2 — Executive Summary
Executive Summary
Each ROI request is multiple independent tasks across different systems and locations. Completing them sequentially creates unnecessary delay, repeat work, and labor costs we can't measure.
1
One request, many independent tasks
Fulfilling a request requires logging into multiple systems and sometimes physically retrieving records. Each system is a separate task — different credentials, different location, different skill. Today one person handles all of them, in sequence, one at a time.
2
Digital and manual worked sequentially
Every request has digital components (EHR records retrievable via Connections) and manual components (onsite imaging, archival retrieval). Because we work them in sequence — digital first, then manual — manual tasks can't begin until digital is done. This creates partial fulfillment with no efficiency gain.
3
Our hypothesis: complete fulfillment tasks in parallel
Split each request into discrete task units and route digital and manual simultaneously — Connections handles digital retrieval while specialists work manual tasks at the same time. Complete fulfillment, faster TAT, measurable cost savings, and visibility into where manual effort goes.
The Ask
Design + Ops to prototype and validate in Q3 · Eng scoping (Connections + ROI Zone) commitment for Q4 if the hypothesis is confirmed
